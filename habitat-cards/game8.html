There are various Habitats in Pokopia that you can restore using Moves and with items and furniture. Wild Pokemon can appear once you make the Habitat, and you'll be able to befriend them!

Event Habitats

Event habitats are unique from the usual habitats found throughout the game, given that you will need to start certain events and purchase specific themed items to build these event habitats in Pokopia.

How to Make All Event Habitats

How Many Habitats are There?

There are 209 Habitats to Build in Pokopia

There are 209 Habitats that you can encounter or build in Pokopia. This number excludes Event Habitats that can be found throughout future events and updates that will be later implemented in the game.

How to Build Habitats

Find Traces of Pokemon

While exploring the land, you may find traces of Pokemon on the ground. Interact with these tiles and you will get a hint about a Habitat, which will be registered in your Habitat Dex.

Do note that it is not necessary to find traces of Pokemon in order to build a habitat. Simply assembling all the required items beside one another will enable the habitat to be built.

Place Items and Furniture

Once you get a hint about a Habitat, you can then use Moves and place the required items and furniture to restore a Habitat. You can check which items and furniture are needed in the Habitat Dex.

You do not need to follow how the items are arranged in the Habitat's icon, but make sure to place them next to each other.

Habitat Will Sparkle if Objects are Correct

If you follow the requirements properly, a sparkle will appear which signals that you have built a Habitat as needed.

Once a Habitat is built, wild Pokemon can appear from the area and take residence there!

How to Befriend Pokemon in Habitats

Create Their Habitat

Once you make a Habitat, you can interact with the area to make a Pokemon that can appear in the respective Habitat show up. You can then befriend them and make them stay.

Pokemon that you befriend may ask for Requests, which are side quests, or teach you Moves that you can use to transform the land and add grass tiles and flowers, destroy stone blocks, cultivate soil, and more!

List of All Pokemon

Occupied Habitats

Note that once a Habitat is already occupied, you need to build another one for a new Pokemon to show up, as only one Pokemon can occupy an existing Habitat. Alternatively, you can move the Pokemon currently residing there to a house or any other available habitat.

How to Move Houses

Weather and Time Will Affect Appearance

Which Pokemon that can appear in a habitat is affected by the current weather and the time of day. This means that different Pokemon may appear during the day or at night, or when it is sunny or rainy.

Some Pokemon are also less likely to appear due to their rarity, so make sure to create various habitats around the map!

All of these information can be checked from the Pokedex and the Habitat Dex.

Pokemon Befriended Can be Moved to a House

Once you have built a house, which is any structure that has four walls and a door, you can fill up the space with at least three pieces of furniture to make it a fully-fledged house.

Once decorated, you can then bring a Pokemon with you, enter the building, and ask them to move into the house.

What are Habitats?

A Place Where Pokemon Can Reside

Habitats are areas where Pokemon that you meet in the game reside. As you explore and revitalize the land, you will create and restore Habitats around the map. Wild Pokemon can then appear and befriend you, and they will call their respective Habitats home!
